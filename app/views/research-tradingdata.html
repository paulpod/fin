Conclusions

How does what we have learned help us built better products?

Summary of key takeaways Implications

Make money - The primary goal of all users is to make money

Users fail - Vast majority fail to achieve this goal

Industry problem - The industry has not made a serious attempt to address this: it is accepted that most clients lose money (previously the industry benefitted from this)

Creating a UX that genuinely improved users chances of success would greatly improve user satisfaction and be a huge differentiator in the market.

Failures known - They are the result of particular attitudes, behaviours and emotions

If we can help users avoid self-sabotaging attitudes, behaviours and emotions we would expect that their trading performance will improve.

Success predictable - The extent to which users have trading knowledge and adopt good trading behaviours (avoid self-sabotaging behaviours and adopt good trading habits) is the primary predictor of their medium and long-term trading success

UX satisfaction - For this reason it is also the primary predictor of their quality of user experience

Improve behaviours - To enable us to improve the quality of experience we need to know what users’ trading knowledge and behaviours are now and create user experiences that help to improve them

The most important things to understand are the trading behaviours and knowledge the extent to which they are committed to improve. Different UX propositions can be built around this.
Key Takeaway

Users want to make money but using trading platforms they mostly fail.

The reasons why they fail are well known.

Addressing will not only create industry differentiator in a largely commoditized market but meet and core and continuing unmet need amongst users.
