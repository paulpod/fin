What is Spread Betting?

Spread betting enables you to trade on the price movements of thousands of financial markets such as shares and indices.

Spread betting is a derivative, which means the market prices are based on or "derived" from the underlying market. You are not directly trading the underlying market. For example, when you place a buy trade on Microsoft you do not physically own the underlying shares. Instead, you are speculating on the future movement of Microsoft shares.

You can use spread bets to speculate on price movements whether the markets are rising or falling. If you go long (buy), your profits rise in line with any increase in that price. If you go short (sell), your profits will rise in line with any market fall.

Similarly, if you go long on the price and the underlying market price falls, you will incur losses.

The Result

You bought at 15791 and sold at 15807, which represents a positive 16-point movement in your favour. At your stake size of £10 per point, nets you a tax free* profit of £160.

* Spread betting and CFD trading are exempt from UK stamp duty. Spread betting is also exempt from UK Capital Gains Tax. However, tax laws are subject to change and depend on individual circumstances. Please seek independent advice if necessary.
