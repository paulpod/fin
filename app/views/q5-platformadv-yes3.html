Short "How to...?" guide on using advanced platform features.

Price Tolerance

Applicable accounts can define the amount of movement in price (slippage) you will accept to execute a trade. The default value for all markets is 2.

The following example illustrates how price tolerance works.

Generic image showing buy and sell prices

The sell price of a market is 10 and the buy price is 12 with the default price tolerance value of 2 used.

You click to sell at 10. The trade is accepted if the sell price is 8 or higher at the time your sell order is received. However, if the price has moved to 7 or lower in the intervening time then the trade is refused.


Changing the Price Tolerance

Generic image of the market information sheet

  1. Open the Market Information sheet of the market that you want to change the price tolerance.
  2. Use the dropdown menu to choose the value of price tolerance you want to use.
